The House

Made in Hatajuku,
kept in a museum of its own

Honma Mokko-jo is a Hakone yosegi-zaiku house in Hatajuku — the mountain village where this marquetry tradition is rooted.

What sets the workshop apart is that it keeps its own museum, the Honma Yosegi Museum, where the craft is shown and preserved. Hakone Yosegi-zaiku is traditional Japanese wood marquetry, designated by Japan's METI as a traditional craft.

Why It's Different

The colours are the wood's own

Koyosegi is fine, small-pattern marquetry — many species of timber set side by side. Nothing here is painted, dyed or stained.

Natural-wood koyosegi

Honey-amber, pale zelkova, olive and deep walnut — each tone is the timber's true colour. No dyes, no stains.

One of a kind

The mosaic is assembled by hand. The pattern varies slightly from piece to piece — no two lids are identical.

Solid walnut body

A quiet, dark frame that lets the bright inlay carry the eye. Built to be handled and kept for years.

The diagonal band

The lid's signature — a running ribbon of geometric koyosegi, set on the bias across the walnut.

Macro · Koyosegi mosaic

Many woods, set side by side

Look closely and the band resolves into hundreds of small geometric pieces — honey-amber, pale zelkova, olive and deep walnut — fitted edge to edge by hand.

Inside

Open it: a deep-red lining

Lift the lid and the box turns warm — the interior is lined in deep red fabric, set against the walnut underside of the lid.

This version is kept plain. There is no mirror and no ring tray — a single soft-lined compartment, room for whatever you choose to keep.

Living with the wood

  • Keep out of direct sunlight.

  • Wipe with a soft, dry cloth.

Good to Know

A few questions, answered

Will my box look exactly like the photographs?
No — and that is the point. Each lid is composed by hand, so the koyosegi pattern varies slightly from piece to piece. No two are identical; treat the variation as part of the craft.
Are the colours painted or dyed?
Neither. Every colour is the natural tone of the wood itself. Koyosegi is built from many species set side by side — nothing is dyed or stained.
Does it include a mirror or a ring tray?
This version does not. The interior is a single deep-red-lined compartment — no mirror and no ring tray.
